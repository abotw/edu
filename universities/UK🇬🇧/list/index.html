    4. University of Southampton

    A respected research university in the south of England, known for engineering, oceanography, computer science, and business. It is active in research partnerships and has a strong international student body. QS Ranking: ~80 (2025) / ~87 (2026) globally.

    5. University of Birmingham

    A Russell Group research university in central England with strong programs in sciences, arts, business, and health. It emphasizes impactful research and offers a broad student experience. QS Ranking: ~80 (2025) / ~76 (2026) globally.

    6. University of Leeds

    One of the UK‚Äôs largest universities, well-regarded for its research and teaching in business, engineering, social sciences, and arts. It combines a vibrant student life with strong industry links. QS Ranking: ~82 (2025) / ~86 (2026) globally.

    A historic Scottish university with strengths in medicine, science, engineering, and humanities. It balances long academic tradition with modern research and global engagement. QS Ranking: ~78 (2025) / ~79 (2026) globally.

    8. Durham University

    A collegiate university in northeast England, known for excellent teaching quality, strong student satisfaction, and rigorous academics in arts, sciences, and social sciences. Its historic setting enriches the student experience. QS Ranking: ~89 (2025) / (~within top 100) (2026).

    9. University of Sheffield

    A research-led university in South Yorkshire, with strong faculties in engineering, sciences, and social sciences. It is known for interdisciplinary research and a supportive campus community. QS Ranking: ~105 (2025) / ~92 (2026) globally.

    10. University of Nottingham

    A major university with a broad range of programs, strong research output, and international campuses. It is known for quality in sciences, engineering, business, and arts. QS Ranking: ~108 (2025) / ~97 (2026) globally.

    Notes on QS Rankings:
